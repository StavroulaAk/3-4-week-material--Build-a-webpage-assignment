Three of the eight bear species of the world!

The Polar Bear

Polar Bear (Ursus Maritimus) is the largest bear of the world, weighing up to 800 kilograms! Their home is the Arctic and places like Canada, Alaska, Russia, Greenland and Svalbard, an Arctic island that belongs to Norway. Thanks to the big population of polar bears, Svalbard is also called the "Polar Bear" island as the population of polar bears is larger than the human one.

Diet Unlike other bears species, they are almost exlusively meat-eaters, eating mostly seals.
Threats Climate change and the lost of sea habitat.
Life span 25-30 years.
Cool to know If you see a yellowish-fur polar bear, you are safe, as this is means it has just eaten and its fur is yellow from the seal fat. If the bear is white, well... run!

Fun fact: Maritinus reminds you of somethings doesn't it? Basically polar bears are classified as marine (sea) mammals!

 

Panda Bear

Panda Bear (Ailuropoda melanoleuca), is a rare and endangered bear. It can weight up to 113 kilograms and that's 1/8 of the polar bear weight! Pandas are born pink and blind and the have 1/900th of their mom's size! Unlike other bears, Panda bears do not hibernate and during winter they move to lower elevations to keep warm. Their home is remote, mountainous regions of central China filled with wet and cool bamboo forests.

Diet Bamboo and bamboo again, up to 20 kg per day.
Threats Poaching, habitat destruction, tourism and farming among others.
Life span 15-20 years for wild pandas and about 30 for those in human care.
Cool to know Pandas spend 10-16 hours per day eating! 99% of their diet is bamboo and 1% consists of eggs, small animals and wheat. And, well, they poo 40 times a day.

Fun fact: Scientists used to believe Panda Bear belongs to the racoon family, but thanks to DNA analysis, Panda found its place among its fellow bears.

 

Brown Bear

Brown Bear (Ursus Arctos), is the bear we are most familiar with as it can be found is small individual populations throughout parts of Europe such as Greece, the coast of Adriatic, Eastern Europe, Turkey and Scandinavia. However, Brown Bear can be widely found in Canada and Western parts of United States, with Russia proudly being a vast home for a healthy Brown Bear population. There around 200,000 worldwide. Brown bears are not only brown. They range in color, from black to white, the so called Kermode Bear. Additionally, they are nucturnal and can weight up to 370 kg.

Diet They are omnivorous, eating plants, berries, fish and small mammals they catch with their big claws.
Threats Currently habitat destruction and persecution, however, they were formerly hunted for their meat and as game trophies.
Life span 15-25 years.
Cool to know Brown bears found in Alaska and Russia are the biggest of their species, being almost as big as Polar bears!

Fun fact: Brown Bears have a really good nose. They can sniff you at a distance of several kilometers.
